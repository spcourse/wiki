This is a list of countries by net goods exports. The list includes sovereign states and self-governing dependent territories based upon the ISO standard ISO 3166-1. The following table shows the value of total annual merchandise exports and imports, expressed in millions of United States dollars (current prices), and the resulting trade balance, according to United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD),[1] World Trade Organization (WTO),[2] and World Bank (WB).[3][4][5]

Countries with positive balance of trade have a trade surplus, countries with negative net exports have a trade deficit.

Exports show the FOB value of goods sent to the rest of the world, valued in current U.S. dollars.

Imports show the CIF value of goods received from the rest of the world, valued in current U.S. dollars.
Trade balance is the difference between exports FOB and imports CIF (or imports FOB when applicable), expressed in current U.S. dollars.
The trade balances of groups of economies are the sums of the trade balances of the component individual economies.
